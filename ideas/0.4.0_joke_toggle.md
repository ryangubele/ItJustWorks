# 0.4.0 - Joke toggle: keep it light, don't impose it

**Grounded in:** *Keep it light* **and** *Respect the player* - the rare case where two principles pull opposite ways, and the honest resolution is to let the player choose rather than choose for them. The joke is the identity; some players won't want it. Both are right at once.

## The tension

Keep it light says the grin lives in the copy, not just the name - `See? It Just Works!`, the whole tone. Respect the player says a utility mod sitting in someone else's 500-hour save doesn't get to force its personality on them. A player running a grim, immersive setup may want the watchdog's function and none of its voice. Withholding the function over a joke would be exactly the ego the last principle warns against - so the answer isn't to pick a side, it's to make the flavor optional and default it on.

## The direction

A single toggle - working name *Dry mode* - that swaps the flavored copy for a plain equivalent while changing nothing about behavior:

| | Default (light) | Dry |
|---|---|---|
| Alert | `In a scene ~3m; blocking others.` / `See? It Just Works!` | `Scene active ~3m; may block others.` |
| EditorID hint | `It Just Works: scenes show as ID numbers...` | `Scenes show as ID numbers...` |
| Hotkey / readouts | already plain | unchanged |

- The toggle affects **copy only** - never detection, thresholds, timing, or any behavior. It's a skin, not a mode.
- Default is light: the identity ships as itself; dry is opt-in.
- The plain strings are translated too, same 43-key parity discipline as the flavored ones - dry mode isn't English-only.
- One toggle for all flavor, not a per-string bikeshed. Audit first how much flavored copy actually exists (it may be small - the alert punchline and the one hint) and decide whether "toggle" or just "quieter defaults" is the right shape.

## Why 0.4.0, ahead of 0.5.0

Pure presentation, fully independent of the detector, so it doesn't wait on observability. Small, safe, self-contained, and it pairs naturally with the 0.2.2 copy pass - you're already in the strings. It slots in while 0.3.0's instrumentation runs as the harder parallel track.

## Open questions

- The label: *Dry mode* / *Quips* / *Levity* / *Serious mode*? It should keep it light without being so cute it needles the people opting out.
- Is a full toggle warranted, or is the alert punchline the only string flavored enough to matter? The audit answers this.
- Where it lives: Diagnostics, or a small Settings header on the Scene page next to the hotkey.
