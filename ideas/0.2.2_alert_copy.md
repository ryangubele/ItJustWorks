# 0.2.2 - Alert copy

**Grounded in:** Keep it light *and* Respect the player. The alert is one of the few places the mod speaks to the player, and its two jobs answer to different principles. Landing *at a glance* is respect for the player's attention - Skyrim buries long notifications, and no one should have to work to read a status line - which is why brevity is the point in the first place. Landing *the joke* is Keep it light. Brevity is the respect; the punchline is the levity. This pass sharpens the first and protects the second, so it earns both.

Make the watchdog notifications as short as language allows. Grammar isn't the priority here; brevity is. Skyrim shrinks long notifications, and a player lingering in a menu has to catch it at a glance, so every word has to earn its place - drop the articles ("a"), drop the filler, telegraphic over correct.

- Today: `In a scene ~3m; blocking others.` / `See? It Just Works!`
- Direction: `in scene ~3m / blocks others`, or tighter, `scene blocking ~3m`.
- When 0.5.0 starts naming which clock tripped, keep that telegraphic too - `scene stuck ~3d game`.

The punchline **stays.** `See? It Just Works!` is the identity, and cutting it to save a word would trade the whole point of Keep it light for a few characters. The trim is for the status line, not the joke - the economy target is everything *around* it; the joke earns its place.

Why not add the mute switch here in the same copy pass? Because splitting it out to 0.4.0 is itself the principles at work, not just scheduling. One small, reviewable release at a time respects the contributor's attention - copy-rewrite and a new translated toggle don't belong in one commit - and shipping the mute switch as its own considered feature respects the player more than bolting it onto a trim. Keep it light without imposing it; just not all at once. See `0.4.0_joke_toggle.md`.

(For reference: the number in the message already reflects the real time-in-scene, not a hardcoded 3 - it tracks whatever the warn threshold is set to. Confirmed in 0.2.0.)
