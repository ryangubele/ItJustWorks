# 0.4.0 - Levity: keep it light, don't impose it

**Grounded in:** *Keep it light* **and** *Respect the player* - the rare case where two principles pull opposite ways, and the honest resolution is to let the player choose rather than choose for them. The joke is the identity; some players won't want it. Both are right at once.

## The tension

Keep it light says the grin lives in the copy, not just the name - `See? It Just Works!`, the whole tone. Respect the player says a utility mod sitting in someone else's 500-hour save doesn't get to force its personality on them. A player running a grim, immersive setup may want the watchdog's function and none of its voice. Withholding the function over a joke would be exactly the ego the last principle warns against - so the answer isn't to pick a side, it's to make the flavor optional and **default it on**.

## The direction

**One toggle. Working name: Levity.** On = flavored copy (default). Off = plain utility copy. One word on a tight MCM - not "Dry mode," not a two-word phrase the player has to parse as a mode of the whole mod.

| | Levity **on** (default) | Levity **off** |
|--|-------------------------|----------------|
| Alert | `scene blocking others ~3m` / `See? It Just Works!` | Plain status only (e.g. `scene blocking others ~3m` without the punchline, or a fully plain pair if audit wants both lines dry) |
| EditorID hint | `It Just Works: scenes show as ID numbers...` | Drop the product wink; keep the instruction |
| Hotkey / most readouts | already plain | unchanged |

- **Copy only** - never detection, thresholds, timing, SEQ, or any behavior. Skin, not mode.
- **Default on** - the identity ships as itself; quiet is opt-in.
- **One control for all flavor**, not per-string bikeshed. Audit how many strings are actually quippy (likely: alert punchline, maybe the EditorID nag). If it's only one line, the toggle still earns its place as a respect valve - and stays one bit of UI.
- Plain and flavored strings both go through the translation tables (when those paths are localized; today many notifies are still English-only - levity doesn't make that worse, and a later notify-i18n pass can carry both variants).

### Why not "Dry mode"

- Two words, sounds like a second product mode (behavior), not a voice dimmer.
- "Levity" is one word, matches the design goal name, and off is obvious (no levity).
- Less MCM width and less player parse on a crowded Diagnostics / Scene page.

"Dry" can stay as internal slang in commit messages; the **label in-game is Levity**.

## Why 0.4.0, relative to 0.5.0

Pure presentation - independent of the detector. Can ship before, beside, or slightly after 0.5.0 without blocking detection. Small, safe, self-contained. Does not wait on two-clock work.

## Open questions

- Exact plain strings after a one-page audit of flavored copy in `Watcher` / `MCM` notifies (and any MCM help that winks).
- Placement: small control near Diagnostics or Uninstall/About - not a new page.
- Whether the first alert line stays identical and only the punchline is gated (minimal), or both lines swap (full dry). Prefer **gate the punchline (+ wink on the hint)** unless audit finds more voice.
